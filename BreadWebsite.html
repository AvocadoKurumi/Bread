Bread Poem 

            From humble grain, a staff of life,
            A simple thing, yet filled with strife.
            The baker's touch, the oven's heat,
            Transforming flour, a golden feat.

            The crust, a crown, a crackling shell,
            Within, the crumb, a story tells.
            Of patient rise, of gentle knead,
            A comfort food, a daily need.

            With buttered warmth, or jam's embrace,
            A canvas bare, a flavor space.
            For soup's companion, sandwich's heart,
            In every bite, a work of art.

            Through famines past, on plenty's shelf,
            A symbol shared, of life itself.
            So break a piece, and share the crust,
            This staff of bread, a bond of trust.
